## Key Observations

1. **Over half of studies have unknown status** (20.8%) or are completed (42.1%), suggesting a significant backlog of research without active follow-up.
2. **Only ~25% of studies are actively recruiting or enrolling**, indicating limited pipeline for new prosthetic innovations.
3. **52.5% of studies are Phase NA** (device/behavioral studies), reflecting the nature of prosthetic research as primarily engineering and rehabilitative rather than pharmacological.
4. **The U.S. leads research volume**, but geographic concentration in urban areas means rural populations are underserved both in research participation and care access.
5. **Only 15 Phase 1 and 4 Early Phase 1 studies exist**, suggesting very limited early-stage innovation pipeline for next-generation prosthetic technologies.
6. **International collaboration is needed** — countries like Jordan, Pakistan, Kenya, Rwanda, and Lebanon have only 1-3 studies each.

## Sample Recent Trial

**NCT07519746 — PROINGA**
- **Title:** Patient Satisfaction and Quality of Life Among Lower Limb Prosthetic Users in Gaza Governorate During Gaza War
- **Sponsor:** Yeditepe University + Al-Azhar University - Gaza
- **Status:** COMPLETED
- **Type:** Observational (Cross-sectional)
- **Enrollment:** 128 participants
- **Period:** Sep 2025 – Mar 2026
- **Location:** Gaza, Palestinian Territories
- **Focus:** Prosthetic satisfaction, quality of life (EQ-5D-5L, SWLS), rehabilitation access in conflict zones
